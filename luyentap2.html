It started like any other weekend — me wandering through quiet streets, looking for another café to add to my list. Ive always loved discovering coffee spots around Vietnam; each place carries a different story, a different shade of calm. That morning, I stopped by a small café hidden behind an old alley. The scent of freshly ground beans filled the air, blending with the soft hum of indie music. I ordered a cup of Vietnamese coffee, found a cozy corner by the window, and let time slow down. Then, as I was lost in thought, the barista walked over with a small wrapped box and a note: “Happy Birthday — from someone who never forgets.” For a moment, I froze. My friend — the one who used to join me on these coffee trails — had somehow found this place, this day, this perfect way to surprise me. I opened the box: a tiny keychain shaped like a coffee cup, simple but warm, just like our friendship. I smiled — realizing that sometimes, the best gifts are not big or loud, but found quietly between sips of coffee and shared memories.
